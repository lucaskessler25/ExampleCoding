The drafting process:

The annual NFL Draft gives the teams the opportunity to infuse their rosters with new talent.

Currently, each of the 32 clubs receives one pick in each of the seven rounds of the NFL Draft (the number of teams drafting has changed over time, and there have been as many as 30 rounds in a single draft).

Once teams are assigned their draft positions, each pick is an asset: It’s up to the club’s executives to either select a player or trade the pick to another team to improve its position in the current or future drafts. Teams may negotiate trades at any time before and during the draft and can swap draft picks or current NFL players to whom they hold the rights.

Each February, hundreds of the very best college football players are invited to the NFL Scouting Combine in Indianapolis, Ind., where executives, coaches, scouts and doctors from all 32 NFL teams conduct an intense, four-day job interview in advance of the NFL Draft. Here is a brief breakdown of the measurable drills:

• 40-yard dash • Bench press • Vertical jump • Broad jump • 3 cone drill • Shuttle run



The hypothesis

In view of the long and extensive selection process, most of the efforts in predicting the draftees happens after the NFL Combine Bootcamp.

This project aims to analyze player's performance throughout the season, and by having all drafts history since 1970, we can create an average player for a team compare which player follows the trend to be selected by each team.

Our thesis is that by having an extensive data on past draftees and their performance, we can compare with NFL prospects and determinate the likelihood of that player to be drafted and by which team.
